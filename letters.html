A Legacy of Love

Letters ✉️

Words written today, meant to be read tomorrow. From Ben and Brittney — to the ones who carry the McGee name forward.

Every family has a story. The McGees are still writing theirs. These letters are part of that story — honest, imperfect, full of love. They're not meant to be polished. They're meant to be real.

🧒
A letter to
Dear Levi
From Ben & Brittney · Written June 2026

You were three years old the first time I met you. You were more interested in the Christmas lights than in whoever "Ben" was — and honestly, that's exactly right. You didn't owe me anything. You were just a little kid doing little kid things, running around, full of energy, completely unbothered.

But by the end of that night, you were on my shoulders. And I drove home knowing — quietly, clearly — that I was exactly where I was supposed to be. Not because of anything I'd done. Because of you.

I chose you, Levi. Not as a responsibility. Not as part of the deal. I chose you because you are one of the greatest gifts I've ever been given. You are funny and stubborn and kind and more perceptive than people give you credit for. You notice everything. You feel everything. That's a superpower, even when it's hard.

You're about to start 2nd grade. You're about to become a big brother. Those are two enormous things, and you are more than ready for both of them. I've watched you grow every single day since that Christmas event, and I have never once doubted that you are going to do something remarkable with your life.

Here's what I need you to know: this family — me, your mom, Baby McGee, Phoenix — we are all yours. Completely. You are not a "step" anything. You are a McGee. You always will be.

We love you bigger than you'll understand for a long time.

— Dad & Mom 🌲
🌿
A letter to
Dear Baby McGee
From Ben & Brittney · Written June 2026

We don't know if you're a boy or a girl. We don't know what you'll look like or sound like or love. We don't know what you'll be afraid of, what will make you laugh, or what you'll want to be when you grow up. We know almost nothing about you — and we are already completely in love with you.

You were the gift we didn't see coming. Your dad and I got married on November 1st. We went to Thailand. We came home. And then, just weeks later, we found out you were on the way. Newlyweds, already expecting. It felt like the universe wasn't wasting any time.

The world you're coming into is a good one. You have a big brother named Levi who is going to teach you things no parent could. You have a dog named Phoenix who will probably sleep on your feet. You have a family at Redemption Tempe that prays for you already. And you have two parents who have been waiting for you — even before we knew we were waiting.

You were made on purpose. Not by accident. Not by chance. By God, for this family, at this exact moment in history. That's not nothing. That's everything.

September 4th can't come soon enough. We'll be ready. And even if we're not — we'll figure it out together. That's what McGees do.

We love you so much already. More than you'll ever fully know.

— Mom & Dad 🌿
💍
A letter to
Dear Brittney
From Ben · Written June 2026

I sent you a hot air balloon invitation more than once. You never replied. And I didn't take it personally — because something told me the timing wasn't right yet. I just waited. Patiently. Quietly. Knowing that if it was supposed to happen, it would.

Then one night, without either of us planning it, we both ended up at the Buffalo Chip Saloon. And I saw you walking toward me, and everything clicked. You said my name, and I blurted out the most honest thing I'd ever said: "BRITTNEY… YOU ARE SO CUTE!"

I have never once stopped meaning that.

You are one of the most remarkable people I've ever known. The way you love Levi — fiercely, completely, without condition — showed me exactly who you were before you ever said a word to me about it. The way you carried Bentley all those years. The way you show up for the people you love, even when it's hard, even when you're running on empty.

I proposed at sunset by the lake. Bentley jumped in the water. Matthias was there in all black with a camera. And you said yes — shocked, wide-eyed, laughing — and I have never been more sure of anything in my life.

You're carrying our baby right now. You're growing a person. In the middle of grief for Bentley, in the middle of life, in the middle of everything — you are doing the most extraordinary thing. And you're doing it with grace.

I am so proud to be your husband. I am so grateful to be building this life with you. Faith. Family. Forever — I mean every word of it.

— Ben 💍
🌲

To Future McGees

If you're reading this years from now — know that this family was built with intention. It wasn't perfect. But it was real, and it was ours, and every chapter of it was worth writing.

"Faith. Family. Forever." — The McGees, Queen Creek, AZ · 2026
