Ben & Brittney McGee · Christian Ceremony

The words we spoke before God and everyone we love.
Written by hand. Meant for a lifetime.

His Words

Ben, To Brittney

To My Wife
Brittney
Written by
Benjamin
"From the moment our eyes met, I knew my life had changed."

From the moment our eyes met, I knew my life had changed. In that instant, I felt an unexplainable peace, something so rare and so deep it touched a place in my heart I didn't even know existed. You have taught me what true love really means.

You have shown me grace, strength, and what it looks like to love without conditions. The way you encourage me when I chase my dreams, and the way you love Levi and me with your whole heart, humbles me every day.

Watching the two of you together has been one of the greatest blessings of my life, and I will spend the rest of my days honoring that love and protecting the family we have built.

Before God and those who love us, I make this vow to you.

I will love you as Christ loves the church, with a heart that chooses sacrifice over pride, service over comfort, and grace over anger.

I will be quick to forgive, slow to anger, and devoted in my devotion.

I will lead our home not by power, but by prayer. Not by authority, but by example.

I will honor you as my equal and my partner in every season of life.

Today, I vow to keep choosing you. Through every adventure, every change, and every moment that feels both new and familiar.

I vow to keep growing with you, to laugh with you when life feels light, and to hold you close when life feels heavy.

I vow to be your teammate in everything — from raising our family, to chasing the life we have always talked about, to building that life brick by brick, hand in hand.

I will guard our marriage with faithfulness, cherish our covenant with joy, and nurture our love so that it reflects the love of Christ to the world.

I will be the one who prays with you, laughs with you, and stands with you through every season.

I will cherish you as my wife, my best friend, and my greatest blessing.

You are my home, Brittney. And standing here in front of everyone we love, I vow to spend the rest of my life making sure you never forget how loved, how seen, and how cherished you are.

I love you, Britt.
As written by Ben
Signed, November 1, 2025
✦
Her Words

Brittney, To Ben

To My Husband
Benjamin
Written by
Brittney
Babe, we made it.

Who would have thought that running into each other 3 years ago at Buffalo Chip would turn into falling madly in love and getting married in front of all our loved ones. God is so good.

You are my best friend, and you have shown me a love so unconditional and so selfless. You've taken the parts of me that I thought were unlovable and still needing healing and turned those things into something beautiful and cherished. You have grown flowers in the darkest parts of me.

"You have grown flowers in the darkest parts of me."

You have far exceeded what I thought love was supposed to be like. The way your family loved me with no hesitation and arms wide open speaks to the love that you so effortlessly give — and that I can't wait to start a lifetime with.

I am forever in love with your soul. With your "good morning beautiful," the way we can be complete goofballs together, how you're always down for a rap battle and a late-night snack. I love how you bring life into every room you enter — and how from the beginning you loved my son as if he were your own. I can't imagine a life without your random serenades and sweet kisses. I can't wait to grow old together, to continue to grow deeper in our faith together.

To hear all the random songs you and Levi will make up together, and for you to lead our house. I can't forget popcorn and M&Ms for the rest of our life together.

I vow to never go to bed angry, to always look to God for how I can better serve you in our marriage.

I vow to honor you and let you lead our family, to always be bone of my bone, flesh of my flesh, and work together as equals.

I vow to be gospel-centered parents and tender-hearted.

To be quick to forgive, to have God's grace — to where I turn to "win the relationship, not the argument."

I vow to lift you and encourage you always, and to never stop fighting for us until our last breath.

I love you, Wife.
As written by Brittney
Signed, November 1, 2025
